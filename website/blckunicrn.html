BLCK UNICRN is an immersive experience platform and creative hub that hosts events recreating concerts from major artists like Taylor Swift to up-and-coming talents. They design immersive spaces that transport audiences directly into an artist's world, offering experiences that mirror music videos or live performances, allowing fans to feel part of the story.

Through the BLCK UNICRN platform, users can enjoy artists' music and videos in an immersive way, using technologies like Oculus for a fully interactive experience.

The Problem

Clear communication of purpose is essential for any platform, ensuring users understand and engage with the brand. Currently, BLCK UNICRN's website lacks a cohesive narrative that conveys its mission and offerings, making it difficult for users to grasp the company's value.

Additionally, images and scaling are not consistently optimized across devices, impacting different screens' visual experience and usability. This lack of responsiveness detracts from the immersive nature that BLCK UNICRN aims to provide, limiting the platform's reach and accessibility.

The Solution

I worked closely with the CEO to capture and format key features, documenting his vision and passion for what BLCK UNICRN’s service would become. I created a comprehensive visual identity guide, establishing fonts, colors, and usage standards to ensure consistency across the platform, ticketing site, and website. Additionally, I conducted a full series of usability tests, gathering insights and feedback to inform iterative redesigns across all website pages.

The Impact

The newly launched site enhanced BLCK UNICRN’s marketing potential, increasing ticket sales for their live immersive experiences. The redesign established a clear information hierarchy, effectively guiding visitors through BLCK UNICRN’s offerings and conveying why this is a must-visit experience.
