    Dark patterns is a deceptive UI/UX decision that uses human psychology to get users to do things they don't want to do, such as buying insurance with their purchase or signing up for recurring bills. There are many types of dark pattern tactics, for example, "Bait and Switch," where the user wants to do a desired action, and in return, the users have to give something of themself, like an email, to access the information they are looking for. Another tactic is "Hidden Cost," where the company advertises to the users a specific cost, but when they go to checkout, additional costs such as shipping would be applied.
